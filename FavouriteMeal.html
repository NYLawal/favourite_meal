Amala Dudu (Yam Flour)

ABOUT

Amala dudu, also called yam flour, is a delicious and nutritious delicacy eaten mostly by the Yoruba ethnic group of Nigeria. Amala has found its way to other parts of Nigeria and Africa and its popularity is increasing by the day.

Amala is made out of flour and there are different kinds, depending on what is used to make the flour. Amala isu is the most common type. The flour used is derived from yam. Amala is high in caborhydrates and packs a lot of calories.

The yam flour can also be called 'elubo'. Yam is mostly white in colour, but it turns into a brownish colour after it has been dried; this gives amala its thick brown colour. Amala can be easily prepared and eaten with any soup of choice (egusi, gbegiri, efo riro, ewedu, etc). To learn how you can present your amala in sumptuous ways, see some options below.

Amala dudu is easy to prepare and doesn't require many ingredients. See the table below to know what you need to start preparing your next amala. Be sure to adjust the measurements as the case may be.

RECIPE

Many a time, people find making amala a little tricky, as it forms lumps easily if not properly handled. Read on to learn how to make lump-free amala.

  • Place a pot on fire.
  • Pour water into the pot and allow to boil.
  • When the water begins to boil, gradually begin to add the yam flour.
  • Turn the flour with a wooden stick very quickly so as to prevent lumps from forming. Turn until smooth.
  • Add little water and leave to cook on low heat for about five minutes.
  • Turn every now and then to ensure even cooking.
  • Bring it down from fire and keep turning until very smooth.
  • Your amala is ready! Pack into nylon wraps or serve as hot. Enjoy!
